    French Basics

    This chapter will go over some basic conversations that you can have and will give you a taste of what French is. For detail the chapter will teach you how to say; Different forms of hello & Goodbye; tell people your name and ask for theirs; Ask how people are doing and tell them how your doing; And have some basic conversation fillers.

    Saying Hello & Goodbye

    French has many words that you can use to greet, just like English. Also just like English the words do make sense, you just need to get use to them. Lets just go over the more simple and more used ways to greet other people in French.

    French Word English Translation Formality Time Specific
    Bonjour Hello Formal/Informal Anytime
    Au Revoir Goodbye Formal/Informal Anytime
    Salut Hi/Bye Informal Anytime
    Enchanté(e) Nice to Meet You Formal/Informal Anytime
    À Bientôt See You Soon Formal/Informal Anytime
    Rebonjour Hello Again Formal/Informal <2 hrs. Since Talking
    Bonsoir Good Evening Formal/Informal Evening
    Coucou Hey Informal Anytime
    Adieu Goodbye Formal/Informal Anytime

    To clear up any misconceptions in the graph above. Lets go over some words in more detail.

    • Rebonjour: If you use Rebonjour make sure that: one, you have talked to that person earlier; And two, the talk was recent. This is like saying hello again to someone after you have talked to them a little earlier.

    • Bonsoir: Bonsoir can be used in the context of both saying hello or goodbye.

    • Coucou: You will use Coucou as a more endearing form of hello mostly around family.

    • Adieu: Adieu is a very formal way to say goodbye. It's commonly found in literature.

    Now that you know some of the different ways to say hello and goodbye people. You can now impress the French with your expertise on greeting. Just know there are still plenty of more ways to say hello and goodbye, these are just the few common ones that may be necessary. Feel free to figure out more ways to say hello and goodbye on your own.

    Saying and Asking Your Name

    Now that you can say hello and goodbye lets get to know the person more and tell them who we are. The sentences below will tell you how to ask for someones name and tell them yours.

    Je m'appelle [Name]

    I'm [Name]

    Comment tu t'appelle?

    Whats your name? (Informal)

    Comment vous appelez-vous?

    Whats your name? (Formal)

    Comment il/elle s'appelle?

    Whats their name?

    The person will now respond with "Je m'appelle [Their Name] since you asked them. Now there are more ways to ask for someones name and who your talking to. Such as, we, they, etc. But you don't need to know them yet. Remember your just learning the basics and if you want to learn them continue. But other than that you can now ask for peoples names and tell them yours, Yay!
