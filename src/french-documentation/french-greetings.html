    Normal Hellos

    French has many ways to say hello just like English. Just like English the words do make since you just need to get use to them. So lets get use to more of the simple ways to say hello! Lets start off with Bonjour, Enchanté(e), Coucou, and Wesh.

    • Bonjour [Hello], is the more formal way to say hello. Use this around elders or people you don't know yet.

    • Coucou [Hey], is a more endearing tone, usually used to greet friends or family causally. Don't use it with people you don't quite know yet.

    • Wesh [Hey], is a more endearing tone, usually used to greet friends causally. It's more used among younger people, not really family.

    • Enchanté(e) [Nice to Meet You], is the formal way to say hello and goodbye. There is a masculine and feminine version so make sure you use the right version.

    Time Sensitive Hellos

    Another ways to say hello does depend on time, be it the morning or near midnight, French has the same time of day logic like English. You can even see a pattern inside of the words, see if you can notice. These are also all formal.

    • Rebonjour [Re-Hello], is the formal way to say hello if its been an hour since you've last talk to each other. This isn't ever used in English so you will have to get used to saying it and when to say it.

    • Ça Fait Longtemps [Its Been a While], is the informal way to greet someone. This is normally used if its been a while since you last meet.

    • Joyeux anniversaire [Happy Birthday], is will be used to greet someone on their birthday. This is both formal and informal.

    • Joyeux Noël [Marry Christmas], is used only around Christmas when greeting others. This is both formal and informal.

    • Bonne Année [Happy New Year], is only used when greeting anyone around New Year. Like some of the ones before its both formal and informal.

    Hellos & Goodbyes

    Most of the ways that you can say both hello & goodbye are time sensitive. So remember to use the ones which are time sensitive at the right times or people may be confused. Bonjour is used tweise in this chapter since its both really important and has many uses. They are also both formal and informal so it doesn't matter who you use them on.

    • Salut [Hi/Bye], is the more informal way to say hello or goodbye or what we say, hi or bye. More used with friends or family when greeting.

    • Bon Matin [Good Morning], is the way to say good morning in the sense of hello and goodbye. You will only use this in the morning.

    • Bonjour [Good Day/Morning], is the way to say good day in the sense of the whole day. This may be also used for good morning, nothing else.

    • Bonsoir [Good Evening], is the way to say good evening in the sense of hello and goodbye. Don't use it during any other time of day.

    • Bonne Nuit [Good Night], is the way to say good night in the sense of hello and goodbye. Like before you can't use it during any other time of day, but you can also use it when going to bed.

    • Au Revoir [Goodbye], is the most common way to say goodbye in French. It can be used for both formal and inform conversation.

    • À Bientôt [See You Soon], is the way to say good morning in the sense of hello and goodbye. You will only use this in the morning.

    • Adieu [Goodbye], is a even more formal way to say goodbye than au revoir. Though this is used more in books for a sense of finality.

    • Au Secours [Bye], is a more playful way to say goodbye literary meaning "To the rescue". That will also make it informals.

    • À La Prochaine [Bye], is the way to say good night in the sense of hello and goodbye. Like before you can't use it during any other time of day, but you can also use it when going to bed.

    • Bonne Route [Bye], is the way to say goodbye to some who is traveling. It literally means "Good road".
